# Touchy Subjects LLC Product Taxonomy

This document organizes Touchy Subjects LLC product concepts into semantic product families. The goal is not just to name products, but to create a brand architecture where every object has a clear behavioral role: grounding, collecting, customizing, sharing, quick regulation, looping, exploring, modifying, or experimenting.

## Brand Lens

Touchy Subjects LLC is best understood as a sensory-object brand: part fidget company, part tactile design studio, part neurodivergent-friendly product system.

Core product principles:

- Quiet, socially discreet sensory interaction
- Organic, holdable forms instead of toy-like plastic language
- Products that invite touch before explanation
- Modular systems that grow over time
- Objects that can be functional, emotional, collectible, and beautiful at once
- Sensory tools that avoid clinical language while still supporting regulation

# Primary Product Categories

## Touch Bases

**Definition:**  
Touch Bases are foundational regulation objects. They are the sensory equivalent of home base: stable, familiar, grounding, and easy to return to. These products should feel dependable rather than novel. Their purpose is to help the user settle, focus, regulate, or self-anchor.

**Product meaning:**  
Daily-carry grounding objects, meeting-safe fidgets, comfort stones, weighted objects, and calm tactile companions.

**Best for:**

- Grounding
- Focus
- Familiarity
- Emotional regulation
- Desk, pocket, classroom, or workplace use

**Products and concepts:**

- Weighted Pocket Pebbles
- Worry Stone Ecosystem
- Focus Companion Object
- Meeting Safe Fidget Series
- Executive Pebbles
- Mood Pebbles
- Warm/Cool Comfort Pebbles
- Simple Infinite Path Pebbles
- Pocket grounding stones

**Design language:**

- Soft pebble silhouettes
- Smooth edges
- Muted colors
- Dense or weighted feel
- Minimal visible mechanics
- Quiet interaction

**Tagline direction:**  
Your sensory home base.

---

## Touch Stones

**Definition:**  
Touch Stones are emotionally meaningful tactile objects. The name draws from the idea of a touchstone as a point of reference, memory, identity, or personal meaning. These products are not only used; they are kept, collected, gifted, personalized, or emotionally attached to.

**Product meaning:**  
Collectible, symbolic, narrative, sentimental, or identity-based tactile objects.

**Best for:**

- Collection
- Personal identity
- Emotional attachment
- Storytelling
- Giftable sensory objects

**Products and concepts:**

- Story Pebbles
- Emotion Pebbles
- Creature Pebbles
- Weather Pebbles
- Artist Collaboration Editions
- Blind Box Collectibles
- Personality Pebble Sets
- Memorial or keepsake tactile objects
- AR Companion Stones
- Sensory Passport collectibles

**Design language:**

- Small object families
- Symbolic marks
- Character-like variation
- Limited editions
- Artist-designed surface treatments
- Collectible packaging

**Tagline direction:**  
Objects with meaning you can hold.

---

## Touch Types

**Definition:**  
Touch Types are customizable sensory systems organized around different ways people prefer to touch, move, press, slide, trace, tap, hold, or fidget. The word type refers to both product type and sensory type: the user discovers what kind of touch works for them.

**Product meaning:**  
Configurable tools, modular fidgets, swappable mechanisms, sensory preference kits, and adjustable interaction systems.

**Best for:**

- Personalization
- Sensory preference discovery
- Modular expansion
- Adjustable intensity
- Build-your-own tactile experiences

**Products and concepts:**

- Adaptive Sensory Difficulty System
- Sensory Generator Cube
- Swappable magnet systems
- Interchangeable rails
- Replaceable TPU spring strips
- Texture cartridge systems
- Modular Sensory Tile Set
- Modular Charm Ecosystem
- Sensory profile kits

**Design language:**

- Visible parts
- Swappable inserts
- Modular panels
- Clear mechanism families
- Multiple resistance levels
- Rebuildable and upgradeable systems

**Tagline direction:**  
Built for your kind of touch.

---

## Touch Upons

**Definition:**  
Touch Upons are shared tactile experiences. The name echoes “once upon,” giving this category a story-based and relational quality. These products are designed for interaction between people, objects, rituals, or narratives.

**Product meaning:**  
Paired objects, collaborative fidgets, story sets, social sensory products, parent-child tools, and ritual-based interaction systems.

**Best for:**

- Shared play
- Connection
- Co-regulation
- Storytelling
- Paired or group interaction

**Products and concepts:**

- Maze Coin Duo
- Multi-user magnetic toys
- Story Pebble Sets
- Parent-child sensory sets
- Desk sensory landscapes
- Sequential interaction toys
- Paired charm sets
- Conversation-safe tactile rituals

**Design language:**

- Linked objects
- Paired parts
- Exchangeable pieces
- Duos, sets, and rituals
- Objects that pass between hands

**Tagline direction:**  
Things we touch together.

---

## Touch 'N Goes

**Definition:**  
Touch 'N Goes are quick-access micro-regulation tools. These are small, immediate, portable products designed for short bursts of sensory input. They are for moments when the user needs a fast reset without setting anything up.

**Product meaning:**  
Keychain fidgets, charm fidgets, pocket sliders, tiny magnetic objects, jewelry fidgets, and fast tactile tools.

**Best for:**

- Quick resets
- On-the-go regulation
- Discreet public use
- Small repetitive actions
- Backpack, pocket, keychain, or jewelry use

**Products and concepts:**

- Silent Hinge Charm
- Detent Pebble Slider
- Magnetic Orbit Disk
- Sensory Jewelry System
- Pocket Rollers
- Tiny Sliders
- Spinner Charms
- Backpack Clip Fidgets
- Magnetic keychain objects

**Design language:**

- Clip-on scale
- Keychain-safe forms
- Jewelry-adjacent details
- Durable closed mechanisms
- Subtle enough for public use

**Tagline direction:**  
Quick touch. Keep moving.

# Recommended Expansion Categories

## Touch Loops

**Definition:**  
Touch Loops are products built around continuous, repetitive, or cyclical interaction. They support rhythm, repetition, flow, tracing, orbiting, and endless return paths.

**Product meaning:**  
Objects that give the hand something repeatable to do without a hard beginning or end.

**Products and concepts:**

- Infinite Path Pebbles
- Magnetic Orbit Disk
- Orbit tracks
- Rotating groove systems
- Endless maze systems
- Loop sliders
- Rolling magnetic tracks

**Why it matters:**  
This should likely become a major product family because repetitive looping interaction is one of the strongest fidget behaviors in the entire concept set.

**Tagline direction:**  
Follow the feeling around again.

---

## Touch Points

**Definition:**  
Touch Points are small, focused sensory input zones. These are not full fidget systems; they are specific tactile moments: press here, rub this, trace this edge, tap this node.

**Product meaning:**  
Micro-interaction surfaces, pressure points, texture islands, tactile buttons, and sensory details that can exist alone or be added to other products.

**Products and concepts:**

- Thumb divots
- Texture pads
- Pressure nodes
- Tactile stamps
- Raised dots
- Soft silicone patches
- Finger landing zones

**Tagline direction:**  
A little place for your fingers to land.

---

## Touch Trails

**Definition:**  
Touch Trails are path-based tactile products. They emphasize finger travel, direction, routes, grooves, maps, and exploratory movement across a surface.

**Product meaning:**  
Objects that invite the user to trace, wander, follow, or discover tactile paths.

**Products and concepts:**

- Topographic stones
- Finger maze pebbles
- Groove maps
- Terrain tracing objects
- Labyrinth surfaces
- Circuit-path stones
- Constellation-path pebbles

**Tagline direction:**  
Trace your way back to calm.

---

## Touch Downs

**Definition:**  
Touch Downs are larger-scale sensory environments where objects settle into a place: desk, shelf, studio, classroom, office, or home. These are not just held; they are arranged, visited, and returned to.

**Product meaning:**  
Desk-based sensory landscapes, modular tactile terrains, display systems, and environmental regulation objects.

**Products and concepts:**

- Desk Sensory Landscape
- Modular sensory terrain
- Magnetic desk ecosystem
- Tactile desktop world
- Display docks for Touch Stones
- Calm station kits

**Tagline direction:**  
A place to land.

---

## Touch Ups

**Definition:**  
Touch Ups are accessories, upgrades, and modifications that change how a product feels, looks, resists, connects, or functions.

**Product meaning:**  
Expansion packs, skins, inserts, faceplates, magnets, springs, shells, and collectible upgrades.

**Products and concepts:**

- Texture skins
- Seasonal shells
- Artist faceplates
- Replacement magnet kits
- TPU spring packs
- Color inserts
- Glow inserts
- Resistance kits

**Tagline direction:**  
Change the feel without changing the favorite.

---

## Touch Tones

**Definition:**  
Touch Tones are atmosphere-based sensory products focused on visual, mood, light, glow, color, reflection, translucency, and calming visual movement.

**Product meaning:**  
Visual regulation objects, light-play products, kaleidoscopic pieces, mood-color objects, and translucent sensory tools.

**Products and concepts:**

- Kaleidoscope Pebble
- Light Diffusion Stone
- Glow Pebbles
- Liquid Motion Pocket Object
- Color lens inserts
- Translucent orbit pieces

**Tagline direction:**  
A mood you can hold.

---

## Touch Labs

**Definition:**  
Touch Labs are experimental products, prototypes, unusual mechanisms, limited tests, and stranger ideas that may later graduate into a core product family.

**Product meaning:**  
R&D, beta drops, mechanical experiments, artist trials, and future-facing concepts.

**Products and concepts:**

- Hidden Mechanism Discovery Toys
- Experimental magnetic systems
- Prototype couplers
- Unusual hinge tests
- AR-linked object experiments
- Digital plus physical sensory ecosystems
- One-off artist experiments

**Tagline direction:**  
The strange little ideas.

# Priority Recommendation

For first physical prototyping, prioritize:

1. Touch 'N Goes — Silent Hinge Charm
2. Touch 'N Goes / Touch Types — Detent Pebble Slider
3. Touch Loops / Touch Trails — Infinite Path Pebble
4. Touch Bases — Weighted Pocket Pebble
5. Touch Upons / Touch Loops — Maze Coin Duo

These have the clearest combination of brand fit, 3D-print feasibility, sensory usefulness, and product-line potential.
