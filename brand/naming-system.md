# Touchy Subjects LLC Naming System

This document defines how product names, category names, collection names, and prototype names should work across Touchy Subjects LLC.

The goal is to keep the brand playful, tactile, emotionally intelligent, and memorable without making the products feel childish, clinical, or gimmicky.

---

## Naming Philosophy

Touchy Subjects names should feel like they belong to a sensory object universe.

They should suggest:

- Touch
- Regulation
- Curiosity
- Comfort
- Motion
- Mood
- Soft mechanics
- Hidden interaction
- Personal meaning

They should avoid sounding like:

- Medical devices
- Therapy worksheets
- Generic fidget toys
- Cheap novelty gadgets
- Overly technical engineering parts

Good names should make someone want to pick the object up before they fully understand what it does.

---

## Core Naming Patterns

## 1. Touch + Familiar Phrase

Use familiar phrases that already carry emotional or behavioral meaning, then bend them toward tactile product language.

Examples:

- Touch Bases
- Touch Stones
- Touch Points
- Touch Trails
- Touch Downs
- Touch Ups
- Touch 'N Goes

Why it works:

- Easy to remember
- Feels conversational
- Creates semantic depth
- Gives categories a reason to exist

---

## 2. Soft Object + Mechanism

Use an organic object word paired with a mechanical or interaction word.

Examples:

- Pebble Slider
- Orbit Stone
- Loop Coin
- Hinge Charm
- Drift Rail
- Pocket Maze
- Worry Disk

Why it works:

- Combines comfort with function
- Keeps the product from sounding too toy-like
- Makes mechanisms feel approachable

---

## 3. Emotional State + Object

Use mood, regulation, or emotional language paired with a holdable object.

Examples:

- Calm Pebble
- Focus Stone
- Grounding Coin
- Drift Pebble
- Overwhelm Stone
- Social Battery Charm

Why it works:

- Connects product to use case
- Makes the object feel personally relevant
- Supports neurodivergent audiences without clinical framing

---

## 4. Motion Verb + Object

Use a gentle movement word paired with the product form.

Examples:

- Slide Pebble
- Trace Stone
- Roll Coin
- Drift Charm
- Loop Disk
- Orbit Pebble
- Glide Tile

Why it works:

- Names the hand behavior
- Makes the interaction understandable
- Works well for product cards and packaging

---

## 5. Secret or Discovery Language

Use names that imply hidden mechanisms, curiosity, or small surprises.

Examples:

- Secret Slider
- Hidden Hinge
- Quiet Lock
- Mystery Pebble
- Discovery Coin
- Little Mechanism
- Soft Machine

Why it works:

- Makes prototypes feel collectible
- Encourages exploration
- Fits magnetic and mechanical designs

---

# Category Naming Rules

## Product Family Names

Product families should usually follow the `Touch ____` format.

Good examples:

- Touch Bases
- Touch Stones
- Touch Types
- Touch Loops
- Touch Trails
- Touch Points
- Touch Tones
- Touch Labs

Use family names for broad product behaviors, not individual objects.

A category should answer: what role does this object play in the sensory ecosystem?

---

## Individual Product Names

Individual products should usually be 2 to 4 words.

Good structures:

- Silent Hinge Charm
- Detent Pebble Slider
- Maze Coin Duo
- Infinite Path Pebble
- Magnetic Orbit Disk
- Weighted Pocket Pebble
- Kaleidoscope Pebble

A product name should answer: what does this object feel like, look like, or do?

---

## Collection Names

Collection names should feel atmospheric, seasonal, emotional, or material-based.

Examples:

- Soft Machines
- Pocket Worlds
- Quiet Little Things
- The Drift Collection
- The Grounding Set
- Stone Garden
- Hidden Mechanisms
- Mood Objects
- Small Rituals

A collection name should answer: what world does this group of objects belong to?

---

## Prototype Names

Prototype names can be more technical, but should still be clear.

Recommended format:

`category-product-mechanism-version`

Examples:

- touch-n-goes-silent-hinge-v01
- touch-loops-orbit-disk-v02
- touch-types-detent-slider-v03
- touch-bases-weighted-pebble-v01
- touch-upons-maze-coin-duo-v01

This keeps repo files searchable while still mapping to the brand system.

# Naming Tone Guide

## Use Words Like

- Pebble
- Stone
- Charm
- Coin
- Loop
- Orbit
- Drift
- Trace
- Glide
- Quiet
- Soft
- Hidden
- Pocket
- Ground
- Mood
- Ritual
- Path
- Terrain
- Field
- Token

## Use Carefully

- Stim
- Neurodivergent
- Anxiety
- ADHD
- Autism
- Therapy
- Regulation
- Disorder

These terms can be useful in educational copy, accessibility copy, or product descriptions, but the product names themselves should generally remain poetic and approachable.

## Avoid Words Like

- Medical
- Treatment
- Cure
- Disorder tool
- Anti-anxiety device
- Autism toy
- ADHD gadget
- Therapy toy
- Sensory aid for disorders

Reason:

The brand can be neurodivergent-friendly without reducing users to diagnoses.

# Recommended Naming Hierarchy

Use this hierarchy when organizing products on the website or in the repo:

```txt
Brand
  Category
    Collection
      Product
        Variant
          Prototype Version
```

Example:

```txt
Touchy Subjects LLC
  Touch 'N Goes
    Quiet Little Things
      Silent Hinge Charm
        Stone Gray / Soft-Close
          touch-n-goes-silent-hinge-v01
```

# SKU-Friendly Naming

For future production, use readable SKU codes:

```txt
TS-[CATEGORY]-[PRODUCT]-[VERSION]-[COLOR/MATERIAL]
```

Examples:

- TS-TNG-HINGE-01-STONE
- TS-TLP-ORBIT-01-GRAPHITE
- TS-TBS-WEIGHT-01-SAND
- TS-TTY-SLIDER-02-SLATE

Suggested category abbreviations:

- TBS = Touch Bases
- TST = Touch Stones
- TTY = Touch Types
- TUP = Touch Upons
- TNG = Touch 'N Goes
- TLP = Touch Loops
- TPT = Touch Points
- TTR = Touch Trails
- TDN = Touch Downs
- TUS = Touch Ups
- TTO = Touch Tones
- TLB = Touch Labs
